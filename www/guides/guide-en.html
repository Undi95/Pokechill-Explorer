📊 Combat Stats ▼

The 6 Base Stats

Each Pokémon species shares the same base stats (BST) which determine actual stats at a given level:

HP - Hit Points
ATK - Physical Attack
DEF - Physical Defense
SPA - Special Attack
SPD - Special Defense
SPE - Speed

Stats determine how much damage a Pokémon deals and takes. Speed also determines move execution speed. Check the BST tab for more details.

IVs - Individual Values

IVs multiply base stats. There are several ways to improve your Pokémon's IVs:

  1. Training - Use the dedicated Training tab: complete 30 battles to gain 2 IVs. The higher your IVs, the stronger the enemies (their level increases).
  2. Vitamins - Use items like HP Up (HP), Protein (ATK), Iron (DEF), Calcium (SPA), Zinc (SPD) and Carbos (SPE) to boost specific stats. You receive 1 random vitamin every 12h (2 per day) when exporting your save (backup).

  3. Genetics - For advanced modifications, visit the Genetics tab.

Division System

Based on their base stats, a Division is assigned to Pokémon. Use this letter to quickly evaluate which Pokémon can perform better in the short term. Check the Division tab for more details:

  • SSS - Grade SSS
  • SS - Grade SS
  • S - Grade S
  • A - Grade A
  • B - Grade B
  • C - Grade C
💡 Higher grade Pokémon have more chances to have a unique ability or move!
🌱 Natures ▼

Nature Effects on Stats

Natures modify a Pokémon's BST stars (base stats). Each nature boosts some stats and reduces others:

Nature Stats ▲ Stats ▼
Adamant ATK +1 S.ATK -1
Modest S.ATK +1 ATK -1
Jolly SPE +1 DEF -1, S.DEF -1
Relaxed HP +1 SPE -1
Quiet HP +1 ATK -1, S.ATK -1
Bold DEF +1, S.DEF +1 HP -1

Obtaining Natures

Natures are obtained through the Training tab:

  • Training - Allows you to reroll the Pokémon's nature (random among available natures based on restrictions)
  • Neutral Mint - Shop item that removes the current nature and resets the Pokémon to "no nature"

⚠️ Important Restrictions

Adamant and Modest have restrictions:

  • Adamant cannot be obtained if the Pokémon's ATK is already greater than or equal to its S.ATK
  • Modest cannot be obtained if S.ATK is already greater than or equal to ATK

General rules:

  • A nature can never make a stat exceed 6 stars
  • A nature cannot make a stat drop below 1 star (minimum 1)
💡 Example: A Pokémon with 5 ATK and 3 S.ATK cannot get Adamant because ATK > S.ATK. However, Modest will be possible!
✨ Abilities ▼

Normal Abilities

Abilities are traits a Pokémon can have. While they are random, some abilities only appear on specific types. Abilities are classified into three categories:

  • Common
  • Uncommon
  • Rare

Discover all abilities in the Abilities tab.

Hidden Abilities (HA)

Hidden Abilities are innate traits specific to the species that can be unlocked in two ways:

  • Use an Ability Capsule (available in the Shop)
  • Via the Pokémon's training

Once unlocked, their effect is permanently active alongside the other ability.

⚠️ Two abilities that do exactly the same thing do not stack (even if it's an HA + a different normal ability with the same effect). Only one will be active.

Ability Memories

Memories are items that allow teaching an ability to a compatible Pokémon:

  • Compatibility - The Pokémon must have a type compatible with the memory. Some abilities are universal (all types), others are reserved for specific types.
  • Usage - Use the memory from your bag, then select a compatible Pokémon in the Pokédex to teach it the ability.
  • Transmission - Once applied, the memory becomes the Pokémon's main ability (slot 1) and can be transmitted via genetics like any other ability.

Where to obtain them:

  • Memory Shop - Basic memories for PB (20-50 PB depending on ability)
  • Apricorn Shop - Random memories in daily rotation for Apricorns of different colors
📈 Experience ▼

Experience Gain

Pokémon gain experience by defeating enemies and share a portion with the team. This applies even if team Pokémon are defeated.

  • Level difference of ±5 levels between your Pokémon and enemy = same XP amount
  • More than 5 levels difference (enemy stronger) = greatly increased XP
  • The limit is set at 10 levels higher, if you defeat a Pokémon 11+ levels higher, you'll get the same as if it were 10 levels higher
  • A Pokémon 10 levels lower than the enemy will receive no XP
💡 Moves are learned every 7 levels.
⚔️ Moves ▼

Move Types

Moves can be changed via right click/long press on a team Pokémon.

  • Physical - Damage based on ATK and target's DEF
  • Special - Damage based on SPA and target's SPD
  • Status - Modify stats or inflict status conditions

STAB - Same Type Attack Bonus

If a Pokémon uses an offensive move of the same type as one of its types, damage is increased by x1.5.

Additionally, single-type Pokémon receive an additional +0.2 STAB!

Cross-Power

If a Pokémon uses an offensive move preceded by an offensive move of a different type, they receive a damage multiplier of 1.3x.

This is indicated by a cross pattern on the affected move's attack bar.

Signature Moves and Egg Moves

Signature Moves - These are moves unique to each Pokémon species. They are automatically learned at level 100 and cannot be inherited through genetics, unless the host Pokémon already has the corresponding Egg Move (in that case, it's considered a normal move).

Egg Moves - These are special moves that some Pokémon naturally know. They can be transmitted to other Pokémon via genetics:

  • Use a "sample" Pokémon that knows the desired Egg Move
  • The Egg Move will be transmitted to the host with normal move transfer probabilities
  • Each Pokémon species has its own specific Egg Move (visible in the Pokédex with the egg icon)

Restricted Moves - Some powerful moves are marked as "restricted". Only one restricted move can be equipped at a time on the active Pokémon.

⚔️ Combat Mechanics ▼

Combat Fatigue

Pokémon lose a very small fraction of their maximum health when attacking. These damage can be reduced by the sum of HP, DEF and SPD stats.

💡 Tankier Pokémon can fight longer.

Status Effects

Some moves inflict status effects (Confused, Burned, Poisoned, Frozen, Paralyzed, Asleep).

  • Last 3 turns by default (except Paralysis which lasts 2 turns)
  • Only one status effect at a time

Buffs and Debuffs

Stat increases or decreases last 3 turns by default (except speed decreases which last 2 turns).

Buffs/debuffs of same magnitude don't stack, but different ones do:

  • +1 then +1 = stays +1 (same magnitude, doesn't stack)
  • +2 then +2 = stays +2 (same magnitude, doesn't stack)
  • +1 then +2 = +3 total (different magnitudes, stack)
  • -1 then -2 = -3 total (different magnitudes, stack)

Weather and Terrains

Some moves can change weather: Sun, Rain, Sandstorm, Hail, Fog, Electric/Grassy/Misty Terrain, Trick Room, Weird Room, Cross Room...

  • Last 15 turns by default
  • Can only be changed after 30 turns (cooldown)
💡 Extension: Some abilities and items make weather last 30 turns instead of 15, and allow it to be reset immediately (without waiting the 30 turn cooldown)! Check weather abilities and terrain items in the Pokédex.
⭐ Shiny & Star Signs ▼

Shiny Pokémon

At a rate of 1/400 (0.25%), Pokémon can appear in shiny version. These chances can be increased:

  • Shiny Pokémon deal +15% damage
  • Visual distinction can be enabled/disabled (does not affect bonus)
  • Shiny does not transfer to evolutions - you must use genetics
Shiny Chance Boosts
Gholdengo - Its hidden ability Good As Gold increases shiny chances by +15% for the whole team
Shiny Charm - Held item. Increases chances by +10% per level (max +50% at level 5)

🧬 Genetics ▼

Overview

Genetics allows modifying a Pokémon beyond what is considered normal for the species:

  • Shiny Mutation - Inherit shiny mutation with 100% chance to family members. Or attempt to propagate a new mutation with a shiny sample.
  • IV Boost - Every operation attempts to increase the host's IVs.
  • IV Inheritance - Inherit IVs from one species to another based on compatibility.
  • Relearning - All moves except the 4 selected ones are reset.
  • Move Inheritance - Inherit moves from the sample.
  • Ability Inheritance - With a Destiny Knot, swap abilities with the sample.

Compatibility

Compatibility determines how much the sample resembles the host:

  • Share one type = +1 level of compatibility
  • Share two types = +2 levels of compatibility
  • Same evolutionary line = maximum compatibility

Test the genetics calculator: Pre-filled Pikachu × Pichu (Shiny)

Power Cost

Power cost determines how difficult it is to modify the host. This influences the time to complete the operation.

A higher division exponentially increases the time required.

💡 Energy Root - Use this item (buyable at shop) to speed up an ongoing genetic operation by 30 minutes.

Genetics Items

Equip an item on the Pokémon before the operation for special effects:

Destiny Knot - Swap abilities with sample
Everstone - Forces new random ability (better chances for rare abilities)
Power Weight - Guarantees HP IV inheritance
Power Bracer - Guarantees ATK IV inheritance
Power Belt - Guarantees DEF IV inheritance
Power Lens - Guarantees SPA IV inheritance
Power Band - Guarantees SPD IV inheritance
Power Anklet - Guarantees SPE IV inheritance
Macho Brace - Multiplies IV inheritance chances by 10
🗺️ Zones & Rotations ▼

Zone Types

The world of PokeChill is divided into several zone types, each with its own characteristics:

  • 🌿 Wild Zones - Standard wild zones where you can catch Pokémon. Different biomes (forest, beach, mountain, etc.) appear based on rotations.
  • 🏰 Dungeons - More difficult dungeons with stronger enemies. Pokémon there are uncatchable, but item rewards are better (evolution stones, gems, TMs).
  • 🎪 Event Zones - Limited-time special zones with exclusive Pokémon and unique rewards. Not always available.
  • 🌌 Mega Dimension - Extremely difficult endgame zone with Mega Pokémon. Requires high level and equipment.
  • 🏆 Frontier Tower - Climbing combat zone with special rotations and division restrictions.

Check the Zones tab to explore all available zones and their details.

Rotation System

Active zones change automatically according to a fixed schedule:

  • Wild & Dungeon - Rotation every 12 hours (00:00 and 12:00 UTC)
  • Event Zones - Rotation every 3 days
  • Frontier Tower - Rotation every 12 hours with different division restrictions
  • Seasonal Zones - Available only during certain periods of the year (Halloween, Christmas, etc.)

Rewards by Zone Type

Each zone offers different rewards:

  • Wild Zones - Catchable Pokémon, basic items, rare vitamins
  • Dungeons - Evolution stones, gems, TMs, rare items
  • Event Zones - Exclusive Pokémon, event items, unique rewards
  • Mega Dimension - Mega Fragments, very rare endgame items, extreme challenges

Exploration Tips

  • Use the Active tab to see currently available zones and time remaining before next rotation
  • Enable notifications to be alerted 15 minutes before each rotation
  • Some zones require special items to be unlocked (tickets, keys)
  • Enemy level increases with your team's level - adapt your strategy!
🏆 Battle Frontier & Raids ▼

Battle Frontier

The Frontier offers difficult battles against powerful trainers. Rewards include Golden Bottle Caps and exclusive Pokémon:

  • 🏭 Factory - Face a passive target Pokémon that doesn't attack (only knows Splash). Inflict maximum damage to increase your score. Rewards every 200 points after 1000 points.
  • 🗼 Tower - Infinite tower with increasing difficulty. Each defeated floor gives +2 Golden Bottle Caps. Maximum 50 item rewards and 100 Golden Bottle Caps. Special ribbon at floor 30.
  • ⚔️ Arena - Face 5 powerful trainers in a row with random terrain effects. Choose a challenge card before starting (1, 2 or 3 terrain effects). Resets every 12 hours.
⚠️ Division restrictions based on rotation (lower divisions are always allowed):
  • Rotation 1: Divisions C, D
  • Rotation 2: Divisions B, C, D
  • Rotation 3: Divisions A, B, C, D
  • Rotation 4 (unlocked after Ultra Lusamine): No restriction (S, SS, SSS)

🌀 Raids T1-T4 (Mega Dimension)

Solo battles against ultra-powerful bosses with 5-minute timer. Require entry items (Mega-Shard/Piece/Chunk/Cluster):

  • Tier 1 - 45,250 HP. Rewards: Exclusive Pokémon, Mega Fragments.
  • Tier 2 - 139,300 HP. Rewards: Exclusive Pokémon, Mega Pieces.
  • Tier 3 - 398,000 HP. Special mechanics (No Mercy, Stealth Rocks, Pledge). Rewards: Exclusive Pokémon, Mega Chunks.
  • Tier 4 - 1,302,000 HP. Unique skills (Omniboost, Wonder Guard). Endgame rewards: Exclusive Pokémon, Mega Clusters.
💡 Bosses with active terrain effects. The 5-minute timer makes DPS crucial. Farm Dimensional Rifts to obtain entry items.
🍵 The Restaurant (Cooking) ▼

Unlocking the Restaurant

The Restaurant is available in the Poké-Mart after defeating Champion Misty in VS mode. It's a place where you can cook dishes that give temporary bonuses to your team.

How It Works

The cooking system allows creating dishes with effects lasting 1 hour:

  • Select 1 to 3 ingredients to compose your dish
  • Each ingredient costs Golden Bottle Caps
  • Each ingredient grants a specific ability to the dish
  • Once cooked, your entire team benefits from the effects for 1h
💡 Limited to 1 dish per rotation: You can only cook once per wild zone rotation (every 12h). Choose your ingredients wisely!

Ingredient List

Available ingredients each grant a specific ability:

Boiled Egg - Wonder Skin (10 GB)
Bread - Big Pecks (10 GB)
Coconut Milk - Hyper Cutter (10 GB)
Fresh Cream - Synchronize (10 GB)
Fragile Bones - Natural Cure (10 GB)
Moomoo Cheese - Unaware (20 GB)
Fruit Bunch - Magic Guard (20 GB)
Fried Food - Rivalry (20 GB)
Mixed Mushrooms - Filter (20 GB)
Pasta - Flame Body (20 GB)
Sausages - Poison Point (20 GB)
Potatoes - Imposter (20 GB)
Instant Curry - Ambidextrous (30 GB)
Pre-cooked Burger - Adaptability (30 GB)

Cooking Strategies

  • Ability stack: Combine multiple ingredients to have multiple abilities active at once
  • Flame Body (Pasta) - Very useful for increasing physical attack damage
  • Magic Guard (Fruit Bunch) - Protects from indirect damage
  • Adaptability (Pre-cooked Burger) - Greatly increases STAB
  • Imposter (Potatoes) - Copies enemy stats
⚠️ Warning: Effects only last 1 real hour and apply to the entire team. Remember to recook before expiration!
